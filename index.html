Hi there!

I am an Oakland-based journalist with interests ranging from data-driven storytelling to investigative reporting. I am passionate about making data more accessible and bringing power to underrepresented voices.

Most recently, I was a data reporter at the San Francisco Chronicle. There, I regularly produced data-driven news articles and graphics about topics like demographics and city services. Here's some of the work I did there:

SFNext Index: Key facts about San Francisco

A dashboard-style collection of data facts and figures for topics including homelessness, housing and crime in San Francisco. I designed the dashboard’s layout, contributed original reporting and produced all Datawrapper graphics seen here.


Poop complaints have swelled in all San Francisco neighborhoods — except this one

I downloaded millions of rows of data from the city’s 311 requests database, implemented cleaning and recoding strategies using R, and produced three Datawrapper visualizations to enhance readers' understanding of the data, including a map of feces-related calls by neighborhood.


This demographic group is a big driver in S.F.’s population decline

I gathered and cleaned data from microdata datasets including IPUMS and the American Community Survey to create Datawrapper graphics highlighting demographic changes in San Francisco.


Before moving to the Bay Area, I was a data fellow with U.S. News and World Report. I also spent a summer in Chicago as a reporting intern covering immigration in the Midwest for Borderless Magazine.

As COVID-19 Spread Through Midwest Detention Centers, ICE Struggled To Provide Accurate Data

I utilized Excel to clean a large dataset, built interactive Flourish visualizations and communicated with government sources. This article revealed flaws in how the U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement presents publicly available data.


As Immigration Laws Change, One McHenry Man Slips Through The Cracks

A long-form feature story examining Chicago's changing immigration laws, and the families they failed to help.
